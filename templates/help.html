How to Use the Fine Arts Semantic Search

Basic Search

Enter keywords related to artworks, artists, styles, or themes in the search box and click "Search" to find relevant artworks.

Examples of search queries:

  • "Impressionist paintings"
  • "Landscapes with water"
  • "Renaissance portraits"
  • "Surrealist dreams"

Contextual Search

Our search engine remembers your previous searches to provide more contextual results:

  1. Check the "Consider previous searches" box to include your search history in the current query.
  2. This is useful when refining your search or exploring related topics.
  3. For example, if you first search for "Monet" and then "water paintings" with history enabled, the system will understand you're likely looking for Monet's water-themed works.

Resetting Your Search

To start a completely new search without any influence from previous queries:

  1. Click the "Reset Search" button in the navigation bar.
  2. This will clear your search history and allow you to begin fresh.

Understanding Results

Each artwork in the results includes:

  • Title: The name of the artwork
  • Artist: The creator of the artwork
  • Museum: Museum name and location (region, country)
  • View in Museum: A link to the official museum page where the artwork is displayed

About Semantic Search

Unlike traditional keyword search, our semantic search understands the meaning behind your words:

  • It can find relevant artworks even if they don't contain the exact words you searched for.
  • It understands relationships between concepts (e.g., searching for "melancholy" might find "The Scream" by Edvard Munch).
  • It's powered by Hugging Face's sentence-transformers, a state-of-the-art natural language processing model.
